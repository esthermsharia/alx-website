Leading
the largest technological
revolution in Africa
For Africans
By Africans



Africa is arguably set to be the next biggest world wide technological hub. The number of innovations and inventions have been on the rise in a continent that for a long period has been labeled as a “dark continent”. Young men and women with a passion for change and a new face of our continent are leading this great revolution. You will be surprised by the rising number of startups that are thirsty for change. Take for example a country like Kenya, where startups including but not limited to the following have been making headlines.
Twiga foods.
M-kopa.
WeFarm.
Sendy.
In western Africa, Nigeria has been in the lead with startups owned and run by Nigerians. They include
Eden.
Ulesson.
Overwood capital.
Bamboo.
WeMove Technologies.
This is just to mention a few countries out the many that the technological wave is sweeping through.



Many organizations are also stemming up with a goal of revolutionizing our continent, These are organizations that are mainly focusing in leadership, science and engineering. They are already producing professionals that are changing the African narrative. Take for example ALU which is dedicated to educating, advising and connecting students to opportunities across the continent and beyond, to foster 21st-century skills and real-world.
Talk of ALX which aims at producing 3 million Ethical and entrepreneurial leaders by 2035.
Some private organizations have also joined the revolution, in Kenya, Equity Bank through their wings to fly program has so far sponsored thousands of students to get quality education within the country and oversees.
African leaders are also establishing a free continental trade area that is set to revolutionize how Africans trade. Apparently 44 African countries have already signed the Agreement. The African free continental trade area is set to avail thousands of opportunities to entrepreneurs.

The revolution is enevitable
